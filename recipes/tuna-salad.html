Tuna Salad

The poor college student deligth

When I was in college money was a bit of a problem. I had to make the most with what my parents sent me, which meant saving money and eating things on sale even if they were out of date. You have to do what you have to do. But there was one recipe that I loved so much I still love to eat it, even now when I still don't have money and still have to eat cheap. My beloved tuna salad.

I never followed any recipe, just mixed what I had. A tuna can, left over salad, and anything I found in my fridge. That is how this baby was born. It is simple, maybe healthy, cheap, quick, and needs no electronics (also had to save on electricity).

Ingredients

  • A tuna can.
  • 2 or 3 Tbsp of mayonnaise.
  • Clean lettuce, a leaft of two. Depends how "healthy" you want it.
  • Garlic salt.
  • 1 or 2 Tbsp of corn, the one that comes in a can.
  • Chilli powder.
  • Salt crackers or toast.

Steps

  1. Open the tuna can and dump the water.
  2. Pour the tunna in a bowl and add mayonnaise.
  3. Tear the lettuce and add the corn.
  4. Season with the garlic salt and the chilli powder.
  5. Done. Eat with crackers or toast.
